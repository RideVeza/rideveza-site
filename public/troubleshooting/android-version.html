Does your phone support passkeys?

A passkey needs Android 9 or newer. Most phones from 2018 onward have this. The steps below show how to check your phone. The exact names, buttons, and screens may look a little different on your phone — look for the closest match.

  1. Open the Settings app

    Open the Settings app on your phone. If you cannot find it, swipe up to see all your apps and look for “Settings”.

  2. Open “About phone”

    Scroll to the bottom and tap About phone. On some phones it is called About device. If you cannot find it, use the search bar at the top and type About phone.

  3. Check your Android version

    Look for Android version. On a Samsung phone, tap Software information first. It must be 9 or higher. If it is, your phone supports passkeys and you are done.

  4. Only if your version is too low: open “System”

    If the version is lower than 9, go back to Settings and tap System. On a Samsung phone there is no System menu — tap Software update in the main Settings list instead.

  5. Tap “Software updates”

    Tap Software updates and install any update you find. On a Samsung phone, tap Download and install. If your phone is already up to date, it is too old to use passkeys. Please speak to your administrator.
